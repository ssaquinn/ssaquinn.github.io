Identity, Values and Strengths

Ethical Decisions:

I find it hard to think of a large ethical decision I have had to make. However, I do believe we are making small ethical decisions every day, and, when combined within our culture overtime, have a massive affect on everyone. Our attitudes affect the attitudes of those around us. Our actions or lack of action can have a positive or negative affect on the lives of others. Depending on what decision is made, they can either contribute to, or ease: discrimination, inequality, environmental harm, mental health/community well-being.

On a more materialistic side, there are practical decisions you can make everyday, such as choosing to buy products from an ethical source, community involvement, lifestyle choices such as diet, transportation type and a decrease in unnecessary consumerism.

I personally try to make ethical decisions by; buying zero-waste products such as shampoo bars, volunteering and donating to community projects and by being aware of my attitudes in social interacts both online and in real life with mindfulness techniques. However, being human, there are still lots of things I could be doing better, such as using a personal vehicle less, consuming less or shopping second hand more. I think what limits me in my ‘ethical living’ is: influences of others, money, time, laziness, selfishness and the need to balance my own well-being by relaxing and not trying to be perfect.

Culture and Identity

I find the word ‘culture’ usually has positive connotations associated with it. However, I personally find it hard to describe my ‘culture’ as I don’t have any customs etc. associated with my background. I came from an abusive family, who’s members lacked a real sense of identity or self-esteem which would manifest in their inappropriate and controlling behavior. So, it’s hard for me to really connect or identify with any ‘culture’. If this has affected me in any positive way, I believe it is in defiance to my families’ world views and behavior. My experience with this, has caused me to develop the values of personal responsibility, social responsibility, empathy, self-awareness, honesty, and kindness.

Thinking Style

According to my Herman Brain Dominance Profile, my thinking style tends towards a creative style of thinking (Experimental Self) and a methodic style of thinking (SafeKeeping Self). I have a moderate amount of social thinking (Feeling Self) and less tendency of analytical thinking (Rational Self).

This means, in a working environment I would do well to be in the design, implementation, overview and organization areas of a project. I would be well suited to be on a team with people who have strong communication, social skills and technical ability to compliment my skill set.

To develop myself to become a more well-rounded thinker, I would need to work on my technical and analytical skills.

Strengths

I think my tendency towards methodic and organized thinking would be very helpful as it helps to build consistency and persistence. Whereas my creative thinking style would be useful in problem-solving.

Working with difficult people

I once was working in a team, where one of the team members would have mood swings and act disrespectfully towards others. She would become angry at team members for illogical reasons. Her behavior would reflect this by ignoring and refusing to engage with the team when the target member was around. I dealt with this by ignoring her behavior, trying to include her without pushing, still maintaining my own positivity and integrity, and trying to separate her and her target. I believe you must be very careful about confronting people who exhibit poor emotional awareness, as it can lead to them becoming defensive and worsening their behavior. If possible, I think it’s best to influence people with positive example.

On the flip side of that, her attitude was never really dealt with, and she continued to bring negative energy to the team, which overtime had a negative effect on everyone’s wellbeing. I think if I was in that situation again, I would still maintain my core strategy, however I would make our manager aware of her behavior to prevent her from escalating. I didn’t make my manager aware at the time because I was attempting to be easy-going (haha I also didn’t want to be a narc). I would also consider talking to the difficult co-worker; however, I would be very careful to do it from a place of kindness (asking her if she’s ok, and if there’s anything I can do to help her) and try not to come across as condescending or anything that would provoke her.
